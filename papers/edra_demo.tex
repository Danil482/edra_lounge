\begin{abstract}
Current LLM sales outreach relies on static, generic prompts, ignoring audience diversity. We introduce EDRA, a closed-loop architecture that dynamically tailors strategies to distinct segments using
cluster-conditional rule induction. EDRA clusters visitor profiles
and matches new users to segment-specific Nearest-Neighbor rules.
If performance drops, an LLM autonomously revises the rules. Validated on 744 real interactions, EDRA significantly outperforms
single-strategy baselines.

Our interactive demonstration explicitly showcases this selfimproving loop. Through the “Attendee View,” conference participants input their professional profiles to engage in real-time,
multi-turn dialogues driven by dynamically assigned cluster rules.
Simultaneously, the “Expert View” dashboard exposes the backend mechanics, rendering live UMAP clustering, continuous rule
consistency tracking, and streaming LLM reasoning during active
policy revisions. In this dual-view setup, each attendee simultaneously experiences personalized AI communication and serves as
live training data, dynamically optimizing the agent’s strategy.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:system}

Current LLM outreach agents personalize content, but rely on static, one-size-fits-all behavioral strategies (e.g., framing, tone) despite highly diverse audiences~\cite{expel2024}. Prior systems add reasoning retrieval or online revision~\cite{trad2024, metaflowllm2025, expel2024, evolver2025, reasoningbank2025}, but none induce or revise rules \textit{per audience segment}, limiting them to inadequate global policies.

Aiming at closing this gap, we introduce \textbf{EDRA} (Experience-Driven Rule Adaptation), a closed-loop architecture featuring \textit{cluster-conditional} rule induction. EDRA dynamically tailors behavioral strategies to distinct audience segments and autonomously revises them upon performance degradation.

The demo pipeline (see Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} for details) comprises four steps:

\noindent\textbf{I. Profile Embedding and Clustering:}
Visitor professional attributes are embedded (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), dimensionally reduced via UMAP, and grouped using HDBSCAN~\cite{campello2013density}. We cluster profiles strictly based on identity, not interaction outcomes.

\noindent\textbf{II. Cluster-Conditional Rule Induction:}
Once a cluster gathers enough interactions, an LLM analyzes them to generate a custom five-slot behavioral rule (framing, tone, opener\_type, word\_target, ask\_size) specific to that audience type.

\noindent\textbf{III. Nearest-Neighbor Rule Application:}
New visitors are assigned to a cluster via $K$-nearest neighbors ($K{=}7$, cosine similarity) weighted voting. The winning cluster's rule governs the agent's subsequent dialogue generation.

\noindent\textbf{IV. Consistency Monitoring and Autonomous Revision:}
EDRA tracks the acceptance rate of each rule. If performance drops below a threshold, the LLM analyzes underperforming episodes and produces a revised rule, ensuring continuous adaptation to demographic shifts over time.

\section{The Demonstration}
We demonstrate an interactive platform where LLM outreach agents dynamically adapt strategies—such as framing, tone, and assertiveness—using real-time audience clustering. A supplementary video~\cite{edra-demo-2026}, source code~\cite{edra-github}, and a public cloud demo~\cite{edra-hosted-demo} are available for reviewer assessment.

The demonstration is presented through two distinct visual interfaces: the ``Attendee View'' for interactive, real-time rule application, and the ``Expert View'' for live monitoring of rule induction and continuous revision.


\subsection{The Attendee View}
The multimedia demo presents a visual-novel interface centered on ``Edra,'' an anime-styled research liaison avatar. The interaction proceeds as follows:

\noindent \textbf{1. Registration:} The attendee enters their email at a full-screen auth gate, establishing identity for the session.

\noindent \textbf{2. Profile Ingestion:} A modal prompts the attendee to paste their LinkedIn profile URL. The system fetches, summarizes, and embeds the profile in real time, assigning the visitor to a cluster via Nearest-Neighbor voting (Section~\ref{sec:system}).

\noindent \textbf{3. Adaptive Multi-Turn Dialogue:} The attendee engages in up to seven turns of conversation. The avatar displays one of twelve emotional states (e.g., \textit{interested}, \textit{skeptical}, \textit{excited}) that respond dynamically to conversation sentiment. Before each reply, Edra's inner reasoning appears as an italic thought monologue---attendees click to proceed, revealing how the agent deliberates. Three response buttons with LLM-generated contextual labels (e.g., ``Tell me about the Monitor product'' or ``How does this compare to manual audits?'') replace static options each turn.

\noindent \textbf{4. Resolution:} An interest gauge tracks engagement from $-5$ (rejected) to $+5$ (accepted). The attendee may also press explicit Accept or Decline buttons. Terminal states trigger a themed popup: ``Collaboration Initiated'' on success or ``Until Next Time'' on decline.
\vspace{-2em}

\subsection{The Expert View}
On the second screen, the demo exposes EDRA's self-improving mechanics through three hover-triggered overlay panels, designed for a secondary display or for curious attendees:

\noindent \textbf{Visitor Profile Panel (right):} Displays the attendee's parsed profile, cluster assignment, and a live t-SNE scatter plot. The attendee's position pulses as a red marker with lines connecting to their $K=7$ nearest neighbors, letting them visually locate themselves among prior visitors.

\noindent \textbf{Rulebook Panel (left):} Lists active behavioral rules with their five-slot configurations, per-rule confidence sparklines, and status indicators (active, revising, deprecated). When a revision is triggered, the LLM's reasoning streams in real time.

\noindent \textbf{Operator Controls (top):} A stats bar shows the episode count, active rules, and revision status. The presenter can press ``Inject Contradiction'' to force contradicting evidence into a cluster on demand, causing the consistency score to drop and triggering a live rule revision preview---the demonstration's most compelling moment.


\section{Evaluation}
To validate EDRA's core hypothesis---that the optimal outreach strategy differs by audience cluster---we framed rule application as a contextual bandit~\cite{li2010contextual} and evaluated it offline on 744 cold outreach interactions extracted from a commercial CRM after filtering warm contacts, autoreplies, and junk messages. Seven message archetypes were discovered by embedding outreach snippets via MiniLM and clustering with HDBSCAN; reply rates across recipient clusters differ significantly ($\chi^2 = 88.93$, $p < 0.001$, Cram\'{e}r's $V = 0.346$).

\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we presented EDRA, a closed-loop architecture that mitigates the limitations of static LLM-based outreach agents through cluster-conditional rule induction and autonomous policy revision. Our interactive dual-view live demonstration successfully operationalizes this framework in a live setting. By allowing attendees to engage in personalized AI dialogues while concurrently serving as online training data, EDRA establishes a scalable and robust foundation for self-improving agents capable of adapting to the inherent heterogeneity and temporal drift of real-world user populations.
